How it's made

The slow part is the point.

01
We ferment for 90 days minimum.

Most commercial hot sauce is made in hours. Ours takes months. Fermentation builds lactic acid the same way a good sourdough does — slowly, converting the pepper's natural sugars into something complex, funky, alive. You can't rush it. The bacteria don't care about your shipping schedule. We put the peppers in the crock and we wait.

02
Peppers from farms within 40 miles.

We source every pepper from growers in the Lowcountry and ACE Basin. Toogoodoo Farm supplies our Reapers. Sandy Island Produce grows our habaneros in the same river plain they've worked for four generations. The terroir of the Carolina coast — the salt air, the black river water, the sandy loam — is in every bottle. That's not marketing. It's agriculture.

03
Every bottle labeled by hand.

We print our labels on a letterpress in North Charleston. Each one gets applied by hand, each batch number written in pen. There's no barcode scanner doing this. If the label is slightly crooked, that's because a person put it there. We think that's a feature.

"No preservatives. No shortcuts.
Just peppers, salt, and time."

Every sauce in the collection uses the same three-ingredient foundation. What changes is which peppers, which farm, which season.

  • Fresh fermented chiles
  • Sea salt (Lowcountry)
  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Filtered water
  • Garlic (seasonal additions)
  • Seasonal aromatics

That's it. Read the label. You'll recognize everything on it.
