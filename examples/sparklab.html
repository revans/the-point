The process

Simple enough to start, complex enough to stick

01
We design the experiment

Every experiment is developed by educators with science degrees, then tested by 30+ kids in the 8–12 range until it produces the right combination of confusion and delight. We don't ship until it works.

02
The box ships to your door

Your kit arrives the first week of each month. Everything is pre-measured and pre-sorted. No hardware store runs, no missing pieces. The box itself is the preparation.

03
Your kid builds and breaks things

The instructions are written at two levels — one for independent explorers, one for parents who want to understand what's happening. Most kids can start alone. All of them will have questions worth asking.

04
The scientist's journal fills up

Every kit includes a journal with observation prompts written the way real scientists write — hypothesis first, then method, then "what surprised me." After six months, your kid has a record of actual experiments they ran.

Questions parents ask

Before you subscribe

Is it safe for kids to do the experiments alone?
Most experiments in Spark Lab are designed for independent completion by kids aged 8–12 without adult supervision — with the exception of anything involving heat or electricity, which includes a "Parent Join" indicator on the instruction card. The safety card in every box explains the actual reason for each precaution, not just the rule. Our chemistry uses food-safe and craft-safe materials wherever possible. When we use something like copper sulfate, we explain what it is, what the hazard level actually is, and how to dispose of it.
What age range does it actually work for?
We say 8–12, and we mean it. Eight-year-olds can complete every experiment, though some will take longer and benefit from a parent nearby. Twelve-year-olds consistently tell us they want to go deeper — which is why every instruction card has a "go further" section on the back with the actual science behind what they observed. We've had subscribers as old as 14 who stay because they find the depth satisfying. We don't recommend it below age 8: the fine motor tasks (measuring, mixing, handling small components) are genuinely difficult for younger children.
What if we miss a month or need to skip?
Monthly subscribers can pause or skip any upcoming shipment up to 5 days before it ships — log in to your account and choose "skip this month." Annual subscribers can pause up to two months per year with no charge. If you forget and the box ships, we can't stop it mid-transit, but we can push your next billing date forward to account for it. No fees, no hoops.
My kid isn't interested in science. Will this change that?
Probably not by itself — and we'd be dishonest if we claimed otherwise. What we've seen in practice is that kids who think they're "not science people" often just haven't seen science that looks like something they can actually do. Electroplating copper crystals is visually dramatic. Slime that behaves like a solid when you punch it is genuinely strange. The entry point isn't "this will be educational" — it's "wait, why does that happen?" Whether that curiosity becomes a sustained interest depends on the kid. We give them a good first encounter. What they do with it is up to them.
Do you repeat experiments, or is every box different?
Every box is different. We have a 24-month curriculum that rotates through chemistry, physics, biology, earth science, and applied math — so if you subscribe for two years, you won't repeat an experiment. After 24 months, we do revisit topics, but always with a different experiment and a more advanced approach that assumes the foundation from the first go-around. We publish a curriculum calendar six months in advance so you can see what's coming.
We live outside the US — do you ship internationally?
Currently we ship to the US and Canada. Canadian orders have a $6 shipping surcharge and arrive 3–5 days later than US orders. International shipping is something we're actively working on — the challenge is customs restrictions on certain chemical components, which vary country by country. If you're outside North America, join the international waitlist and we'll let you know when your region is available.
