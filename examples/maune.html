Pattern
Before
Fabric.

Every piece in the collection begins as a problem in pattern. Not a sketch — a question about how cloth distributes weight, how a seam placed three millimetres left of convention changes the way a coat sits on a moving body. The answer is the garment.

We make eleven pieces per season. That number is not a production constraint. It is a commitment: eleven decisions made completely, rather than thirty made adequately. Each piece is closed — no re-runs, no archive reissues. When the season ends, it ends.

The client who finds us has usually stopped acquiring things. She is choosing them. The distinction matters to us as much as it does to her.
