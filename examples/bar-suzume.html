Suzume
The Bar

Twelve seats.
Open since 1962.

Three at the bar. Nine at tables. The ceiling beams are cedar, blackened over sixty years by cigarette smoke. A brass trumpet hangs on the wall. A musician placed it there in 1967. He never came back for it.

The bartender has worked here since 1978. There is jazz. There is no cocktail menu. The bar serves one bottle of Nikka From the Barrel per evening. When it is gone, it is gone.

Not silence. Jazz is playing.
But quiet from everything else.

The Whisky

One bottle.
Per evening.

Nikka From the Barrel. Blended malt and grain whisky, bottled at 51.4% without chill-filtration. When the bottle is finished, the bar stops serving whisky. This is not a policy. It is how the evening works.

The bar does not substitute another bottle. It does not extend last call. The whisky runs out when it runs out, and then there is a different kind of quiet.
