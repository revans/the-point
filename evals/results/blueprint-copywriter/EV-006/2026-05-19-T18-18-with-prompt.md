**Truth anchors I'm pulling from the brief:**
- "Fourth time that Friday" — specific frequency with a specific day, which means this is a Friday-at-4pm emotion, not an abstract inefficiency
- "Why does this still require me?" — exhaustion, not anger. The reaction that goes into the headline.
- "Playing messenger" — the Reddit vocabulary. The exact phrase to steal.

Framework: **PAS** for the hero. **JTBD** for features. The job isn't "use the portal" — it's "stop being the copy-paste layer between client and file."

Tone: Direct, warm, peer-to-peer (freelance designer audience).

---

## 1. Three Headline Variants

| Angle | Headline |
|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | You're not a designer right now. You're a copy-paste layer. |
| **Benefit-led** | Client feedback on the file. Not in your inbox, not in Notion. |
| **Direct** | A client portal where your clients leave comments on the deliverable — and you stop moving them. |

**Recommendation:** The curiosity headline. "Copy-paste layer" is the Reddit vocabulary with the recognition turned up to maximum — every freelance designer reads that and feels the specific Friday frustration. It stops scrolling because it names the thing they haven't named yet.

---

## 2. Subtitle (adds new information)

Paired with the curiosity headline:

> Your clients are leaving feedback — in emails, in DMs, in screenshots of screenshots of your PDF. Give them one link. They comment on the file. You open your dashboard and it's already there.

**Why this earns it:** The headline names the problem. The subtitle shows the before-state (emails, DMs, screenshots of screenshots — escalating absurdity that designers will recognize) and then the after-state in two short sentences.

---

## 3. CTA Options

| Placement | CTA |
|---|---|
| Hero primary | Send your first client link |
| Secondary / low commitment | See how a client sees it |
| After pricing | Set up your portal in 5 minutes |

**"Send your first client link"** names the immediate action after the click. It's also the product thesis compressed to a verb — the entire value prop is that a link replaces the manual loop.

---

## 4. Feature Descriptions (Outcome-First)

**Comments land on the file, not in your inbox**
> Your client opens a link, sees the design, and leaves a note directly on the frame they mean. You open your dashboard and the feedback is already there — no copying, no translating, no wondering which version they were looking at.

**Every round of revisions in one thread**
> Revision 1 comments. Revision 2 comments. The approved final. All in sequence, in the same place. Not in three email threads and a voice note that arrived after you already made the change.

**Clients click a link. That's it.**
> No "create an account" screen. No password your client forgets before they've left a single note. The friction stops at your end, not theirs.

**You know exactly what's been seen**
> You can see when your client opened the file. Which means "I haven't had a chance to look" is no longer an ambiguous statement — and "I just need a few more days" comes with context.

---

## 5. Tone Calibration

Freelance designer audience → **Direct, warm, peer-to-peer.** Write like one designer talking to another at the end of a long Friday.

What to avoid:
- Corporate formality ("our platform enables seamless collaboration")
- Vague benefits ("save time," "work smarter")
- Any sentence a project management SaaS would put in their hero

The signal: if a Notion or Monday.com marketing team could use it unchanged, cut it.

---

## 6. Nav Labels + Footer Tagline

**Nav labels:**
```
How it works  |  Pricing  |  Log in
```

Functional, no jargon. "How it works" passes because it tells the visitor exactly where they're going and promises an explanation — which is what a tool with a non-obvious mechanism needs.

**Footer tagline:**

> Built for the fourth time you copied feedback from an email.

This couldn't appear on any other product's footer. It's the exact moment from the brief. A designer reads it and knows — without needing the headline — exactly what problem this solves and who built it. That's the whole job.
