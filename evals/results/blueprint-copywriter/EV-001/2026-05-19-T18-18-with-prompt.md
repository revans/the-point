Good setup — but I can't write your copy yet without running the interview first. Here's why: without your answers, I'll write the same three headlines every invoicing tool already has. The interview questions are what separate your copy from a placeholder.

You said no customers yet, so we're in **Branch B**. Three questions. Answer them as specifically and messily as you want — the useful stuff is in the details.

---

**Question 1: The frustration moment**

Describe the moment you knew this was a real problem. Not the idea — the actual moment of frustration. What were you doing, how many times had you done it, and what did you say to yourself?

*(Example of the level of specificity I need: "I was manually formatting a PDF invoice in Figma for the third time that week and I sent it to the wrong client because I had 12 tabs open.")*

---

**Question 2: The five-minutes-before sentence**

What does a freelance designer say out loud — to themselves, to a Slack channel, to nobody — five minutes before they'd desperately want your product?

*(This is the first thing a reader should nod at when they land on your page. It's short. It's specific. It's already in their head.)*

---

**Question 3: The 11pm Reddit post**

Write the Reddit post your ideal customer posts at 11pm. Not polished. Venting. What subreddit is it in? What's the title? What does the body say?

*(This is the most useful question. "r/freelance, 11:14pm: 'Is anyone else losing an entire Monday to invoice cleanup? I've tried FreshBooks and Wave and I still spend 3 hours fixing things before I send anything."* — that post has five things I can build your homepage from.)*
