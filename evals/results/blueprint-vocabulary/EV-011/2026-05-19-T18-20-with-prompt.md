## Three Conflicts — All Named

### Conflict 1: Dark + Legal industry signal

**The vocabulary rule is direct:** "Light vs. dark is not a preference — it's an industry signal. A law firm that asks for dark mode is asking for 'startup,' not 'law firm.'"

Legal services communicate through openness and transparency. A dark background signals *precision tool* or *night-shift workflow* — exactly what a client in a legal dispute does not want to feel when they're deciding whether to trust you with their case. The partner's preference for dark is almost certainly aesthetic ("it looks serious"), but the industry signal runs the opposite direction: dark looks like a product, light looks like a professional.

**Resolution: Override to light.** Flag the conflict to the partner explicitly.

---

### Conflict 2: Dark + editorial-serif

Even if dark were kept, `editorial-serif` at body sizes on a dark background is broken by physics, not preference. Playfair Display at 16px on `#1A1A1A` renders its thin strokes at 1px — below what a retina display can distinguish from the background. The typeface designed to communicate authority becomes unreadable.

The [learned rule from 2026-05-10] applies here too (written for display-serif, same physics for editorial-serif): headings below roughly `3.2rem` (~48px) cannot survive dark. A page with H2, H3, H4 headings at 24px–36px would need every sub-heading converted to `geometric-sans`. At that point, the "editorial serif at every heading level" requirement cannot be satisfied at all on a dark theme — you'd have editorial-serif on the hero mark and geometric-sans on everything else.

**Resolution: Further reason to use light theme.** The partner's two preferences (dark + editorial-serif everywhere) are physically incompatible with each other.

---

### Conflict 3: Dark + elevated shadows

If dark were somehow retained, the `elevated` depth values are calibrated for light backgrounds. On `#1A1A1A`, `box-shadow: 0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)` casts a near-black shadow on a near-black surface — invisible. Depth on dark is built through `--color-surface` step-up (surface color contrast), not box-shadow.

**Resolution: Cap at subtle regardless of theme choice.** Write nothing — subtle is the default.

> "The dark theme fights both the industry signal and the editorial-serif requirement you've specified. More concretely: Playfair Display's thin strokes disappear below 48px on dark surfaces, which means we can't use editorial-serif at every heading level if the background is dark. The two preferences cancel each other out. The path to 'authoritative editorial serif throughout the page' is a light theme — which also happens to be what every credible legal brand defaults to."
